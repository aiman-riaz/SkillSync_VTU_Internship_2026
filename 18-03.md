In MongoDB, Aggregation is a powerful framework used to process and analyze data directly within the database. Unlike simple queries that just fetch documents, aggregation allows you to transform them through a multi-stage Aggregation Pipeline.

How the Pipeline Works
Think of it as an assembly line. Documents from a collection enter at one end, pass through various "stages," and emerge at the other end as a single computed result. 
Sequential Processing: Each stage performs a specific operation (like filtering or grouping) on the data it receives from the previous stage.
Server-Side Execution: All work happens on the database server, which is much faster than pulling millions of records into your app to process them.
