Model-View-Controller (MVC) is a structural design pattern that separates an application into three main components. This "divide and conquer" approach makes code easier to maintain, test, and scale by ensuring each part has a single, clear responsibility. 
The Three Components
Model (The Data):
Manages the data logic and business rules.
It communicates with the database, performs calculations, and provides data to the Controller.
Analogy: The Chef who knows the recipes and manages the pantry.
View (The Interface):
What the user sees (HTML, CSS, JSON).
It displays information provided by the Model and sends user actions (clicks, form submits) to the Controller.
Analogy: The Plated Meal presented to the customer.
Controller (The Brain):
The intermediary between the Model and the View.
It processes incoming requests (via middleware!), fetches data from the Model, and tells the View what to display.
Analogy: The Waiter who takes your order, tells the chef what to make, and brings you the food.
