A Time-To-Live (TTL) index is a specialized single-field index that MongoDB uses to automatically remove documents from a collection after a certain amount of time or at a specific clock time.


At the heart of this feature is a background thread called the TTL Monitor. 
Scanning Frequency: The monitor runs approximately every 60 seconds. It scans for collections with TTL indexes and identifies documents where the indexed date field, plus the specified expireAfterSeconds value, is less than the current time.
Deletion Logic: When it finds expired documents, it deletes them as a background task. Because this process is asynchronous, there is often a slight delay (up to 60 seconds or more depending on system load) between the actual expiration and the deletion from disk.
Performance Management: To prevent the database from slowing down during massive cleanup, the monitor deletes documents in batches. Starting in MongoDB 6.1, these are often "batched deletes" to improve efficiency.
