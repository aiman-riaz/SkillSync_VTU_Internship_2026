Real-world organizations use MongoDB to solve complex data challenges where traditional relational databases often struggle, such as handling unstructured data, massive scaling, and real-time processing.

Top Industry Use Cases
E-commerce & Product Catalogs: eBay and Walmart use MongoDB to manage billions of records with varying attributes (like size or colour) that don't fit into rigid tables.
Internet of Things (IoT): Bosch uses it to process real-time telemetry from millions of connected devices and industrial tools to improve diagnostics and maintenance.
Customer 360 & Service: MetLife consolidated data from over 70 legacy systems into "The Wall," a single-view application that gives agents a unified look at 90 million customers.
Content Management: Forbes and The New York Times use MongoDB's flexible schema to store diverse digital assets (text, images, video) and scale rapidly when stories go viral.
Gaming: Electronic Arts (EA) and SEGA rely on it for real-time player statistics, leaderboards, and managing complex in-game inventories that change constantly.
Big Data & Biometrics: The Aadhar project in India manages one of the world's largest biometric databases, using MongoDB to store demographic data and images for over 1.2 billion people.
