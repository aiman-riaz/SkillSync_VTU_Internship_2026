Weighted Average Formula
The theory behind a weighted score is Feature Importance. Not every GitHub activity is equal—for example, a "Commit" usually shows more effort than a "Star."
Significance: By assigning weights (like 40% for code, 20% for activity, etc.), you manually defined what "success" looks like in your app.
The Cap (Normalization): Capping the score at 100 is a UX best practice called clamping. It prevents "power users" with 10,000 commits from breaking your scale or making the UI look buggy with a 1,000% score.


Multi-Dimensional Analysis
By splitting the engine into 5 categories, you moved away from a "vanity metric" (like total followers) and toward a Holistic Profile.
This is common in "Credit Scoring" or "Gaming Stats." It allows you to rank users on different axes—like someone being a "Great Collaborator" (PRs/Issues) even if they aren't a "Heavy Coder" (Commits).
